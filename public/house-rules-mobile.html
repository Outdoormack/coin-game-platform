Game Night
House Rules
An Official Supplement to the Constitution of the Third Space Treasury
Issued by the Treasury · Effective Immediately · Subject to Amendment by Popular Chaos
The Treasury recognizes that its citizens gather, from time to time, to engage in recreational competition of the tabletop variety. Whereupon such gatherings occur, coins shall not merely observe — they shall participate.

§ 1 — The Ante

At the start of each Game Night, every attending citizen contributes one (1) coin to The Pot — a central collection of unclaimed coins visible to all.

The Pot

Pot coins sit unclaimed in the center. When awarded, the recipient scans immediately. Pot coins not awarded by end of night go to the Final Scramble (§ 5).

§ 2 — Awards

The following awards are granted throughout the evening — once per game round unless noted.

🏆 Victor's Claim 1 from Pot
Win a game round outright.
😂 Moment of the Night 1 from Pot
Group votes for the funniest, most chaotic, or most memorable moment of the round.
🗡️ Best Betrayal 1 from Pot
Social deduction games only. Group votes for the most devastating bluff, play, or double-cross of the round.
📡 Bullseye 1 from Pot
Wavelength only. Closest guess to the target in the round.
😬 Consolation Prize 1 from Pot
Last place player receives a coin. Nobody goes home empty-handed. It is still embarrassing.
🔥 Hot Streak Steal from player
Win two consecutive rounds in the same evening. Steal one coin directly from any other player — bypassing the Pot.
On Voting

Subjective awards are settled by quick group vote — majority rules, host breaks ties. Thirty (30) seconds max. The Treasury does not convene deliberation panels.

§ 3 — Claiming Your Award

  1. Scan the NFC coin with your phone.
  2. Enter your name (or confirm if saved).
  3. Select Earned (Pot coin) or Stolen (player steal).
  4. Write a story. The chaos deserves documentation.
  5. Take a photo if the moment demands it.

All Game Night claims count toward season score, leaderboard rank, streaks, and badges.

§ 4 — The Hot Streak Rule

Win two consecutive rounds and you may steal one coin directly from any player present. The target may refuse — but refusal costs 5 points from their Treasury score, enforced on the honor system.

Running from a Hot Streak is legal. It is also embarrassing.

§ 5 — The Final Scramble

After the last game, remaining Pot coins go to the center. The host counts down from three. At zero — free-for-all. First to scan it, claims it.

There are no rules during the Final Scramble. Only winners and people who didn't move fast enough.

§ 6 — Story Requirements

Every Game Night claim must include a story. The app will not stop you from skipping it. Your fellow citizens will know.

§ 7 — General Provisions

7.1 Host Authority. The host holds final interpretive authority. All rulings are final until the next Game Night, at which point nobody will remember anyway.
7.2 No Coin, No Ante. Players without a coin may not participate in the Pot. Hot Streak steals and consolation awards remain available at host discretion.
7.3 Pot Shortfall. If the Pot runs dry, remaining awards are fulfilled via player-to-player steals by group consensus.
7.4 Game Compatibility. These rules layer on top of any tabletop game without modifying that game's rules. Awards distribute at the next natural break if timing conflicts.
7.5 Amendment. Rules may be amended by majority vote. Amendments take effect at the next Game Night.

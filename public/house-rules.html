Game Night
House Rules
An Official Supplement to the Constitution of the Third Space Treasury
Issued by the Treasury · v2 · Subject to Amendment by Popular Chaos
The Treasury recognizes that its agents gather, from time to time, to engage in recreational competition of the tabletop variety. Whereupon such gatherings occur, coins shall not merely observe — they shall participate. These House Rules govern the conduct of Treasury coins at official Game Nights and establish the procedures by which coins are wagered, scanned, and awarded in the course of said festivities.

§ 1 — The Ante & The Mystery Pot

At the commencement of each official Game Night, every attending agent shall contribute one (1) coin to The Pot — a shared bowl of coins, shuffled and anonymous. No one knows whose coin is whose. No one knows what rarity or effect hides inside.

The Pot is the engine of the night. It dispenses points, reveals secrets, and rewards the bold.

How The Pot Works

Pot coins are shuffled together so no one can identify individual coins. When a player earns a scan from the Pot, they reach in blind, scan the coin, and discover what they pulled — the rarity and effect are revealed on the spot. The physical coin goes back in the Pot. Points are yours. The mystery stays alive.

§ 2 — Awards & Bonus Scans

Throughout the evening, players earn bonus scans from the Pot for the following achievements. Each award is granted once per game round unless otherwise noted. Award winners scan a random coin from the Pot — points are recorded, but the physical coin stays in the bowl.

The host may also award bonus scans at their discretion for moments that defy categorization. The host is the Grand Arbiter — their generosity, like their judgment, is final.

Award Trigger Reward
🏆 Victor's Claim Win a game round outright 1 bonus scan
😂 Moment of the Night Group votes for the funniest, most chaotic, or most memorable moment of the round 1 bonus scan
🗡️ Best Betrayal (Social deduction games only) Group votes for the most devastating political move, bluff, or double-cross of the round 1 bonus scan
📡 Bullseye (Wavelength only) Closest guess to the target in the round 1 bonus scan
😬 Consolation Prize Awarded once per night to the player who suffered the most — fewest points, eliminated first, or most spectacularly defeated 1 bonus scan
On Voting

Subjective awards (Moment of the Night, Best Betrayal) are settled by quick group vote — majority rules, host breaks ties. Voting shall take no longer than thirty (30) seconds. The Treasury does not have time for deliberation panels.

§ 3 — Scanning a Pot Coin

When a player earns a bonus scan, they claim it immediately using the Treasury app:

  1. Reach into the Pot and pull a coin at random — no peeking, no choosing.
  2. Scan the NFC coin with your phone.
  3. Discover what you pulled — rarity, effect, the whole reveal.
  4. Select Earned as the claim type.
  5. Write a story. The chaos deserves documentation.
  6. Return the physical coin to the Pot.

All Game Night scans count toward season score, lifetime score, leaderboard rank, streaks, and badges — same as any other claim.

§ 4 — End of Night: The Coin Return

At the conclusion of the evening, every player who anted a coin goes home with a coin. No one leaves empty-handed.

The host decides the redistribution method:

Option A — Random Draw. Pot coins are shuffled. Each player draws one coin blind. Simple, fast, and the mystery carries to the very end.

Option B — Winner's Pick. Players who earned the most bonus scans during the night pick first from the Pot. Remaining players draw in any order. Ties are broken by the host or by rock-paper-scissors.

Either way, every agent walks out with a coin in hand.

§ 5 — Story Requirements

Every coin scanned at a Game Night must include a story entry. The Treasury app will not stop you from skipping it — but your fellow agents will know, and they will judge you accordingly.

Stories should capture the moment: the round, the play, the chaos, the betrayal. Future leaderboard archaeologists deserve context. Write something worth reading.

§ 6 — General Provisions

6.1 — Host Authority. The host of any Game Night holds final interpretive authority over these rules. If there is ambiguity, confusion, or dispute, the host decides. The host's ruling is final and non-appealable until the next Game Night, at which point nobody will remember anyway.

6.2 — No Coin, No Problem (Mostly). Players who arrive without a coin may still play games and earn bonus scans from the Pot — they just don't ante and don't take a coin home at the end of the night. The only way a coinless player leaves with a coin is by winning one through normal gameplay outside of Game Night.

6.3 — Game Compatibility. These rules are designed to layer on top of any tabletop game without modifying that game's rules. If an award conflicts with ongoing gameplay, it is distributed at the next natural break.

6.4 — Amendment. These rules may be amended at any time by majority vote of attending agents. Amendments take effect at the next Game Night. Constitutional amendments require the same process as described in the Constitution of the Third Space Treasury.
